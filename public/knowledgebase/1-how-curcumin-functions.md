Curcumin works at the **cellular level** to target signalling pathways and perform corrective measures — which is why it has the potential to address autoimmune diseases **without major side effects**, unlike traditional allopathy drugs that act on specific targets such as enzymes, receptors, or ion channels.

## How It Differs from Modern Drugs

Modern drugs are highly specific, targeting **single receptors or metabolic pathways**. Curcumin, a polyphenol, has been shown to target **multiple signaling molecules** while also demonstrating activity at the cellular level — this is what supports its wide range of health benefits.

## Key Properties

- **Anti-oxidation** and **anti-inflammation**
- **Antiviral** effects
- **Anti-pulmonary fibrosis** support
- **COPD** management effects

## Athletic & Recovery Benefits

Curcumin may also help manage:

- **Body fatigue** and gym exercise-induced inflammation
- **Muscle soreness** — enhancing recovery and performance
- **Sports injuries** — curcumin supports ligament wear-and-tear repair
- **Tissue remodelling** and rebuilding
